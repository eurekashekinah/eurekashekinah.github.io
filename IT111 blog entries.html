LESSON 1

Lesson 1 introduces fundamental concepts in computing, covering computer systems, hardware, software, networking, and the societal impact of computing. Students develop foundational skills in problem-solving and logical thinking.

LESSON 2

Lesson 2 explores the workings of computers, problem-solving, and the creation of applications. It offers paths to become a programmer, designing software, or a system analyst, understanding and enhancing computer systems.

LESSON 3

Lesson 3 delves into the information processing cycle, encompassing Input, Processing, Output, and Storage. Computers follow these steps to effectively process data.

LESSON 4

Lesson 4 explores the complexity of a computer system, which includes hardware, software, peopleware, and dataware. It emphasizes processing and managing information effectively.

LESSON 5

Lesson 5 emphasizes computer security, crucial for ensuring the integrity and availability of information in a digital environment. It involves safeguarding systems and data from unauthorized access, damage, or disruption.

HTML AND CSS

HTML and CSS, though challenging, form the backbone for creating and styling web pages. HTML structures content, defining elements like headings and paragraphs, while CSS handles presentation and layout. Mastering these fundamental web technologies is essential for creating aesthetically pleasing and well-structured websites.
